Would you Trust a Robot to Understand Logic?

Instructions

  • We have trained three robots to read and determine the semantic relation between a paragraph (the “Premise”) and a sentence. The relation can either be “entailment”, which means the sentence is consistent with the paragraph, or “contradiction”, when the sentence is contradictory to the information in the paragraph, or “neutral”, when the sentence is irrelevant.
  • While making their determination, the robots will highlight a segment of the paragraph that got their attention. Your task is to determine whether you can place your trust in the judgment; the robot smart enough to pay attention to the right part of the paragraph is the one better at semantic logic.
  • First, we will show you examples of the three semantic relations and how each robot selects the texts, so you can see how each robot works. After that, you will Segment nine questions. In each question, we will show you segments highlighted by these three robots, and ask you to identify the robots that highlight each segment.
  • Based on how they work, some robot may appear more trustworthy than others. To indicate how much you trust these robots, you have $100 to bet on them and to explain your reason. You will place your first bet after reading the examples, and have a chance to adjust your bet after every three questions.
  • If you are ready, please proceed to read the examples.
